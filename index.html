Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 19, 2026  |  App: Resumify – Resume Builder & CV Maker

This Privacy Policy describes how Resumify ("we", "us", or "our") handles information in connection with your use of our mobile application Resumify – Resume Builder & CV Maker (the "App"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

1. Information We Collect

We do not directly collect or store any personal information from our users. The App operates locally on your device — your resume data, work experience, education history, and other content you enter remains stored exclusively on your device and is never transmitted to our servers.

However, our App uses Google AdMob to serve advertisements. AdMob automatically collects the following information to deliver and optimize ads:

  • Device information (device model, operating system, app version)
  • Advertising ID (Google Advertising ID / GAID)
  • Approximate location (derived from IP address — city/region level only)
  • IP address
  • App usage and ad interaction data

2. How We Use Your Information

The information collected through AdMob is used for the following purposes:

  • To deliver and facilitate the delivery of our App services
  • To deliver targeted advertising content to users
  • To determine the effectiveness of advertising campaigns
  • To identify usage trends and improve the App experience
  • To protect our services and prevent fraudulent activity
  • To comply with applicable legal obligations

3. Disclosure of Your Information

We disclose (but do not sell) certain information to the following third-party service provider:

  • Google AdMob — for the purpose of serving banner, interstitial, and rewarded advertisements within the App. AdMob may use your Advertising ID, device information, approximate location, and usage data to deliver personalized ads.

Google's Privacy Policy can be found at: https://policies.google.com/privacy

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to any other outside parties.

4. Cookies, Beacons & Tracking Technologies

Our App uses the following tracking technologies through Google AdMob:

  • Targeting / marketing cookies — to serve relevant ads based on user interests
  • Beacons / pixels / tags — to measure ad impressions and performance
  • Click redirects — to direct users to an advertiser's page when an ad is clicked

You can reset your Google Advertising ID or opt out of personalized ads at any time through your Android device settings: Settings > Privacy > Ads.

5. Data Retention

We do not directly store any personal data. Data collected by Google AdMob (such as Advertising ID, device information, and approximate location) is retained by Google in accordance with their data retention policies. Approximate location derived from IP address is typically retained for up to 9 months, and ad interaction data for up to 26 months. For more information, please refer to Google's Privacy Policy.

Data collection ceases when you uninstall the App from your device. You may also reset your Advertising ID at any time through Android device settings.

6. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction of information. Your resume data is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to our servers. Google AdMob applies enterprise-level security measures to any data it collects on our behalf.

In the event of a security incident involving unauthorized access to personal information, we will notify affected users and relevant authorities within the timeframes required by applicable law (e.g., within 72 hours under GDPR).

7. Children's Privacy

Our App is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through our App, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

8. International Data Transfers

Google AdMob is a US-based service. By using our App, information may be transferred to and processed in the United States or other countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom (UK). These transfers are governed by:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — approved by the European Commission for international data transfers. Google's Data Processing Terms are available at: https://business.safety.google/adsprocessorterms
  • EU-US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) — Google LLC is certified under the EU-US DPF, UK Extension to the EU-US DPF, and Swiss-US DPF.

9. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you
  • Right to deletion — request deletion of your personal information
  • Right to opt out — opt out of targeted advertising via your Android device settings
  • Right to correction — request correction of inaccurate personal information

To submit a privacy request, please contact us using the details in the Contact section below.

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete personal information, and the right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information. We do not sell your personal information. To exercise your California privacy rights, please contact us.

11. European & UK Privacy Rights (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or UK GDPR. The legal bases we rely on for processing personal information include:

  • Legitimate Interests — for advertising, fraud prevention, and improving the App
  • Legal Obligation — where processing is required by applicable law
  • Vital Interests — to protect the vital interests of any person

You may lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. EU users may contact their national Data Protection Authority. UK users may contact the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at https://ico.org.uk.

12. Other Regional Privacy Laws
